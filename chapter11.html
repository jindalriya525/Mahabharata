Chapter 11: Stri Parva

Chapter Introduction

Stri Parva is the eleventh book of the Mahabharata, in which the grief, pain, and deep sorrow of the women following the war are described. This section gives voice to the mourning of the wives, mothers, and sisters of fallen warriors. Whereas earlier the war represented heroism and valor, here the consequences of war are revealed through the eyes of women.

Core Themes

  • Lamentation of the women for the fallen warriors on the Kaurava side
  • Gandhari blames Shri Krishna for the destruction
  • Stories of grief from Draupadi and the five queens
  • Public mourning over the death of Bhishma, Drona, Karna, and others
  • Yudhishthira takes moral responsibility for the sins of war
  • Final funeral rites of the Kuru warriors are performed by Yudhishthira
