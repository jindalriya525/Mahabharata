The Karna Parva of the Mahabharata begins from the tenth day of the great war and spans till the seventeenth day. This Parva primarily focuses on the legendary rivalry and battle between Karna and Arjuna. After the fall of Bhishma and Dronacharya, Karna is appointed the commander of the Kaurava army. This section reflects KarnaтАЩs valor, his final battle, and a major turning point in the Kurukshetra war.

ЁЯЫбя╕П Karna Becomes Commander-in-Chief

Following the death of Dronacharya, Duryodhana was deeply sorrowful. He then appointed his closest friend Karna as the commander of the Kaurava army. Karna took an oath to defeat the Pandavas from the very first day of his command. Shalya was made his charioteer, although ShalyaтАЩs loyalty remained with the Pandavas in spirit.

Karna launched massive attacks on the Pandava army. His arrows injured many mighty warriors. He defeated Nakula, Sahadeva, and even Yudhishthira тАУ but he did not kill them, remaining true to the vow he had taken. After humiliating Yudhishthira, Karna prepared for his final showdown with Arjuna.

The fateful day arrived when Karna and Arjuna faced each other on the battlefield. Lord Krishna served as ArjunaтАЩs charioteer, while Shalya guided KarnaтАЩs chariot. Though Karna was defenseless, he still tried to fight. On KrishnaтАЩs command, Arjuna invoked the Anjalika Astra and struck Karna, ending his life. This marked a decisive moment in the war, ending the last strong hope of the Kauravas.

Karna was a legendary warrior тАУ known for his generosity, patience, and unwavering sense of duty. Though born to Kunti, he lived unaware of his true lineage for most of his life. With KarnaтАЩs death, the Karna Parva comes to an end. The war now enters its final phase. The fall of Karna was not merely the death of a warrior тАУ it marked the fall of an ideal, a promise, and an unwavering commitment.
